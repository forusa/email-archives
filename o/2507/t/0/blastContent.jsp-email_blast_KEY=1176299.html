Friends,

START may be a good start, but there is much more to do.

Recently, President Obama signed a treaty with Russia that promised both nations would reduce their nuclear stockpiles by one-third. The treaty, once ratified by the Senate, will extend the Strategic Arms Reduction Treaty (START), which expired in December.

The White House says the treaty is a landmark in progress to the reduction of nuclear weapons, and leaders of both nations pledged further work toward additional reductions.

Nonetheless, a closer look reveals that this new treaty leaves superpowers with many nuclear options. The Fellowship of Reconciliation USA encourages you to act now to eliminate the nuclear threat to your city, your country and your world.

Take action now!

Sign the petition to President Obama and the United Nations. Your signature will be among millions collected on this and similar international petitions presented at the May 2010 Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty Review Conference at the United Nations in New York.

Write your senators to support ratification of the new START treaty. Although this agreement is only a stepping-stone, it's an important step toward a world free of the threat of nuclear war. Read more about why this ratification is so important.
