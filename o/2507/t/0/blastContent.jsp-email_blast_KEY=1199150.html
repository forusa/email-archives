Dear Friends,

This month's elections results sobered progressive activists, with political conservatives making dramatic gains in Congress and statewide races. But peacemakers should not lose hope: positive social change is still happening at the grassroots level across the nation.

In “Renewing the Movement,” the new Fall 2010 issue of Fellowship magazine, a diverse cadre of writers highlight creative examples of justice work, proposals for building a stronger peace movement, and challenges for this political praxis moment.

Read selections from this exciting new issue now.

Are you a subscriber to Fellowship? If so, you have even more to look forward to! Legendary activist Vincent Harding, the primary author of Martin Luther King, Jr.’s renowned “Time to Break Silence” April 1967 speech in New York City, granted Fellowship an exclusive interview. Read his in-depth “We Are Creating a River” conversation with young FOR leader Lucas Johnson. Other print exclusives include: Iraqi war veteran Josh Steiber’s analysis of the seventh anniversary of the U.S.-Iraq conflict; poetry by Alice Walker; and reviews of two dozen new books and films.

Not yet a subscriber? No problem. Subscribe today ($40.00) and we’ll send you the fall issue, as well as another year’s worth of great content. And in the meantime, enjoy articles from this and past issues online.

It’s important to take the lessons of this election cycle to heart, consolidate gains, and most of all, maintain momentum and hope in the face of these political setbacks. Fellowship, in its mission to provide news of the peace movement at home and abroad, can help you stay connected to the movement and offer evidence that progress for peace continues to be made.
