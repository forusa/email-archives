Selections from our holiday collection of greeting and postcards

Five children, silhouetted in white with falling snow and walking with lanterns. Along the bottom of this postcard are words by Emily Greene Balch: "We have a long, long way to go. So let us hasten along the road of human tenderness and generosity. Groping, we may find one another's hands in the dark."

The radiant sun and a happy cloud look down upon green hills and blue waters. Along the bottom of this postcard are the words, "Shalom -- just to be is a blessing ... just to live is holy," by Abraham Heschel, late leader of the Jewish Peace Fellowship.

A Russian style icon transformed by rich red colors of the Mexican desert, showing Mary and the baby Jesus. The inside of this greeting card is blank, and on the back is a commentary about the artist and the artwork. Based on a seventeenth century rendition of the Kykko Icon by Simon Ushakov.

A golden-hued bas relief of the holy family in the Bethlehem church on the site where Jesus is said to have been born. This greeting card is from a photo by former FOR executive director Doug Hostetter. The inscription inside, from a ninth century carol, proclaims peace.

A golden menorah on a dark background. Rays of light emanate from the menorah, intersecting to form stars of David and beyond that to embrace the earth. Inside this greeting card are lines from the song "Not by Might -- Not by Power."

Elegant portrait of plums and pear (five greeting cards of each fruit). Blank inside, with the back providing a couple of lines from a poem, with an explanation, about wassailing the trees.
