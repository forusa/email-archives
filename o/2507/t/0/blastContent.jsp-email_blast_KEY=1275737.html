The Fellowship of Reconciliation invites you to two events in Washington this week. The first is a Congressional-sponsored briefing on Capitol Hill:

Obama in Latin America: The U.S. Response to the Region’s Leftward Shift

Featuring

  • John Lindsay-Poland, Research Director at the Fellowship of Reconciliation
  • Mark Weisbrot, Co-Director of the Center for Economic and Policy Research
  • Laura Carlsen, Director of the Americas Program at the Center for International Policy
  • Miguel Tinker Salas, Professor of Latin American Studies at Pomona College

Honorary Co-host: Representative Raúl Grijalva (D-AZ)

Thursday, May 30, 2013
10:30 a.m. – 12:00 p.m.
1539 Longworth House Office Building
Washington, DC

In the past decade and a half, Latin America and especially South America have experienced unprecedented political change. This briefing will look at how the U.S. administration has responded to the region’s shifting political dynamics and offer recommendations for improving U.S. relations with the rest of the hemisphere. The panelists, in Washington to participate in the 2013 Latin American Studies Association International Congress, will discuss the following issues:

  • U.S. relationships with the new left-leaning governments in the region
  • The Impact of U.S. Regional Security and Counternarcotics Policy
  • Regional institutional changes resulting from the collective efforts of the hemisphere’s new governments
  • Major areas of difference between various Latin American governments and Washington
