Fellowship of Reconciliation


Come together to share interfaith prayers, teachings, singing, and fellowship at this FOR-sponsored event! This will be a multi-generational inclusive family.

This Sunday, Sept. 15, 2:00 PM
Temple Beth Emeth
100 Academy Road, Albany, NY
Get directions to this event.

It is a non-political event, and we welcome your group/congregational banners and non-political signs of peace. Join other community members to reaffirm common roots.

Suggested attire: white clothing and comfortable shoes.

The walk will be a total of 2.2 miles, and its starting point is the parking lot of Congregation Beth Emeth.

That will be followed by brief stops at: Corner New Scotland/South Lake Ave., Quaker Meeting House, Church of God of Prophecy, St. Mary’s & St. George’s Coptic Orthodox Church, First Lutheran Church/CACC, Morning Star Missionary Baptist Church, Victory Christian Church, The Shrine Church of Our Lady of the Americas, Masjid As-Salaam.

Sponsors: Ahmadiyya Muslim Community, Albany Friends Meeting House, Artists’ All-Faith Center, Bethlehem Neighbors for Peace, Capital Area Council of Churches, Children at the Well, Commission on Peace and Justice, Congregation B’Nai Sholom, Congregation Gates of Heaven, Cornerstone Protestant Campus Ministry UAlbany, Fellowship of Reconciliation, First Lutheran Church, First Reformed Church, Interfaith Story Circle, Justice Committee of Sisters of St. Joseph of Carondelet Albany Province, Karma Triyana Dharmachaka, Kateri Peace Conference, Labor Religion Coalition of NYS, Masjid As-Salaam, Muslim Solidarity Committee, Muslim Students Association, Palestinian Rights Committee, Peace Action, Russell Sage College Spirituality Center, Saratoga Neighbors for Peace, Saratoga Peace Alliance, Tendai Buddhist Institute, The Interfaith Alliance of NYS, The Interfaith Center UAlbany, UAlbany Hillel, UAlbany Indian Students, Association, UAlbany Newman Catholic Association, Underground Railroad History Project of the Capital Region, Upper Hudson Peace Action, Veterans for Peace Chap. 10.
