Fellowship of Reconciliation


We can't thank you enough for being a member of the Fellowship of Reconciliation, Friends.

When you signed the FOR Statement of Purpose, you joined a now-century-long timeline of people working for peace and justice through active nonviolence.

However, I've noticed you're not currently receiving updates from FOR, other than notifications of events in your area.

Friends, we want to support your efforts for peace and justice in your community by providing resources, tools, news of events, and inspiration through our ongoing nonviolent narrative. Yet, if you'd prefer not to receive updates of FOR's work by email, we'll respect that. I know that inboxes are often quite full!

If you're open to giving it a try, however, I'd love to keep you in the loop of the work FOR is doing nationwide. For instance, in the past few weeks we've sent email about:

  • FOR's annual Peace Awards, currently open and accepting nominations!
  • A video retrospective of FOR's first century, recorded from our centennial events last year.
  • An exclusive invitation to speak with the International FOR's coordinator, Rev. Lucas Johnson, a long-time FOR-USA member from Atlanta.
  • An online conversation on music and activism with Rev. Osagyefo Sekou, FOR Bayard Rustin Fellow and organizer in the Movement for Black Lives.
  • A report-back from our Give Refugees Rest campaign, which gathered in Washington, D.C., and met with staff from the Speaker of the House's office.

Do these sound like things you'd like to hear about? If so, I encourage you to sign up for our regular email updates.

FOR tends to send out email no more than once or occasionally twice per week. You can change your subscription preferences at any time, or unsubscribe completely -- no hard feelings!

Would you like to hear more about FOR's ongoing work? Please sign up for our email updates.

Thank you again for everything you've done in support of the Fellowship of Reconciliation, in your community and nationwide!

In peace,

Linda E. Kelly
Director of Communications
Fellowship of Reconciliation
